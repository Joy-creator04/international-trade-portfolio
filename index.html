About This Portfolio

This portfolio documents my exploration of key concepts from the International Trade course at Dong-A University. Through readings, class activities, spreadsheets, research projects, and presentations, I developed a deeper understanding of how countries trade, specialize, and interact in a globally connected economy.

The projects below demonstrate how economic theories can be applied to real-world trade issues such as tariffs, trade dependency, comparative advantage, and supply chain disruptions.

Final Reflection

Throughout this course I learned that trade is far more than simply exchanging goods between countries. International trade is connected to economic growth, specialization, global supply chains, resource distribution, and government policy. The COVID-19 pandemic demonstrated how dependent modern economies have become on international trade and highlighted the importance of resilient supply chains.
