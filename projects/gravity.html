Gravity Model of Trade

Exploring how economic size and geographic distance influence international trade flows.

Project Overview

The Gravity Model predicts trade between countries using two main variables:

  • Economic Size (GDP)
  • Distance Between Countries

Larger economies tend to trade more because they produce and consume more goods and services. Countries that are geographically closer usually trade more because transportation costs are lower.

Evidence

The activity required collecting GDP information, measuring distances between countries, calculating predicted trade values, and comparing predictions with actual trade data.

Analysis

The model showed that countries with larger economies generally trade more with each other. Distance reduced trade intensity because transportation costs, shipping time, and logistical challenges increase as countries become farther apart. However, the activity also demonstrated that factors such as trade agreements, political relationships, and cultural connections can influence trade beyond GDP and distance alone.

What Did I Learn?

This project helped me understand that international trade patterns can be explained using measurable economic variables. I learned how economists use mathematical models to predict trade behavior and why real-world outcomes sometimes differ from theoretical predictions. The activity improved my ability to analyze economic data and understand trade relationships between countries.

Real-World Application

Governments, economists, and international organizations use Gravity Models to:

  • Predict future trade flows
  • Evaluate trade agreements
  • Analyze transportation costs
  • Study globalization patterns
  • Forecast economic integration
