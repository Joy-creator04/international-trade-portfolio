Comparative Advantage & Specialization

Understanding why countries trade and specialize according to opportunity cost.

Project Overview

Comparative Advantage explains why countries benefit from trade even when one country can produce more of every good. Instead of focusing on total production, countries should specialize in the product with the lowest opportunity cost and trade for the rest.

This activity required calculating absolute advantage, comparative advantage, specialization decisions, and production possibility constraints.

What I Learned

Before this activity I believed countries should always produce everything they are best at. However, I learned that comparative advantage is based on opportunity cost rather than absolute productivity. This concept demonstrated why specialization and trade can increase overall economic efficiency and benefit multiple countries simultaneously.

Real-World Application

  • Bangladesh specializes in garments.
  • South Korea specializes in advanced manufacturing.
  • Saudi Arabia specializes in petroleum products.

International trade allows countries to focus on their strengths while importing goods produced more efficiently elsewhere.
