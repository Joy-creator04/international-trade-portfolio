Trade vs Autarky

Analyzing whether countries benefit from self-sufficiency or international trade.

Project Overview

Autarky refers to a country attempting to be economically self-sufficient without depending heavily on international trade. For this activity I analyzed trade patterns and evaluated whether a country was operating under autarky or actively participating in global trade networks.

Evidence

The activity required examining trade relationships, imports, exports, and economic indicators to determine whether the country was economically isolated or integrated into global markets.

What Did I Learn?

This activity demonstrated that complete autarky is rare in the modern world. Most countries depend on international trade because specialization and exchange improve efficiency, productivity, and consumer choice.
